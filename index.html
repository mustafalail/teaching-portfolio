Welcome to My Teaching Portfolio!

Dr. Mustafa Al Lail is an Associate Professor in the School of Engineering at Texas A&M International University (TAMIU). Throughout his career, he has had the opportunity to learn from and contribute to various institutions. This experience includes serving as visiting faculty at esteemed institutions like Oak Ridge National Laboratory and Colorado State University and holding a faculty position at Seattle University before joining TAMIU.

Dr. Al Lail's academic journey has been enriching. He holds Master's and Ph.D. degrees in Computer Science from Colorado State University, where he benefited from the guidance of distinguished professors: Dr. Indrakshi Ray, a cybersecurity expert, and Dr. Robert B. France, a leader in software engineering research. His foundation began with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering from the well-regarded King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals in Dhahran, Saudi Arabia.

Teaching Accomplishment Highlights

I am pleased that you are taking the time to review my teaching portfolio. I have carefully compiled this document to showcase my teaching philosophy, accomplishments, and contributions to student learning. I invite you to explore the various sections of my portfolio at your own pace. I have made an effort to present the information clearly and concisely. However, please feel free to reach out if you have any questions or need further clarification on any aspect of my work. Feel free to explore the tabs at the top of the page to easily navigate through my portfolio. Below, I have shared some highlights of my teaching accomplishments that I hope you'll enjoy reading!

My commitment to teaching excellence is reflected in a strong record of accomplishments.

  • Student Success:I have successfully mentored 48 undergraduate research assistants, guiding their projects and fostering their development as independent researchers. Five of these students secured internships and permanent positions at DOE National Laboratories, eight went on to graduate schools for Master's and Ph.D. programs, and the rest have obtained excellent positions in the industry. My courses consistently receive high evaluations from students, with an average rating of 4.7 out of 5, reflecting a high level of student satisfaction.
  • Curriculum Development: I have played a pivotal role in the development of new programs, including the B.S. in Computer Engineering and the B.S. in Computer Science, ensuring that our students receive a cutting-edge and relevant education. I have also developed 12 new courses from the ground up, incorporating innovative teaching methodologies and utilizing cutting-edge technologies to enhance student learning.
  • Professional Development: I am committed to continuous professional development, actively participating in numerous teaching certificates and training programs to refine my pedagogical approaches and enhance student engagement.
  • Technological Innovation: I am a strong advocate for the effective integration of technology in education. I have effectively utilized platforms like Top Hat, Echo360, and zyBooks to enhance student engagement, facilitate interactive learning, and personalize the learning experience for each student.
  • Leadership and Mentorship: I have actively mentored colleagues, sharing my expertise in teaching and technology. I have also served as a mentor for undergraduate research projects, fostering their growth and success.

These accomplishments demonstrate my dedication to providing a high-quality and engaging learning experience for my students, and my commitment to continuous improvement in my teaching practices.

Synergetic Activities and Relationship to the Mission of TAMIU

As a Minority-Serving Institution, Texas A&M International University (TAMIU) serves a diverse student body, with 89.3% identifying as Hispanic, 76% coming from low-income backgrounds, and over 51% being first-generation college students. Through instruction, research, and public service, TAMIU's mission to improve the quality of life for citizens in the border region, the State of Texas, and national and international communities resonates deeply with my professional values. I joined TAMIU because of the strong alignment between my mission as a professor and the university's mission and strategic plan, which emphasizes visionary academic programs and faculty, empowered student success, and advancing research excellence. My research, teaching, and service activities all focus on empowering students to become agents of positive change.Â 

My contributions to TAMIU's mission are multifaceted. A significant achievement has been my involvement in establishing the new Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Engineering, which has seen a remarkable growth of 165% since its launch in the 2020-2021 academic year. This program addresses the need for skilled engineers in our region and beyond. Furthermore, I have mentored over twenty-five undergraduate research projects, providing invaluable research experience to diverse students, including many Hispanic scholars, and preparing them for future success in graduate school or the workforce. My commitment to research excellence is also evident in my efforts to expand collaborative and interdisciplinary research opportunities, collaborating with scholars from TAMIU, R1 research universities, Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and international partners. These collaborations advance knowledge in my field and provide enriching opportunities for our students.

These synergistic activities demonstrate my dedication to TAMIU's mission. I am committed to continuing my work at TAMIU, which has become a second home, and further contributing to the success of our students and the communities we serve. My portfolio reflects my commitment to TAMIU and its mission, and I welcome any questions or requests for further information. I am grateful for the support of the TAMIU family and look forward to serving our students and the university for many years to come.
